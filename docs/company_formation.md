# TTI Fire — Company Formation Decision

**Context:** Tech startup (fire alarm troubleshooting, inspection, and monitoring mobile app + IoT platform). Planning to grow, hire, and potentially raise investment.

---

## Entity Type: C-Corp (not S-Corp, not LLC)
- VCs and investors **require** C-Corp — they cannot legally invest in S-Corps
- Enables stock options (ISOs) for employees and co-founders
- Industry standard — accelerators (YC, etc.) and lawyers expect it
- S-Corp is for small profitable businesses with no investor plans — not us

---

## State: Delaware (not Florida)
- **Industry standard** — investors expect Delaware; deals sometimes require reincorporation if you're not
- Specialized business court (Court of Chancery) — predictable, investor-friendly rulings
- Since we operate in Miami, we register as a **foreign corp in Florida** too (~$125/year)
- Extra cost: ~$500–600/year vs Florida-only — negligible vs $5K–15K legal cost to reincorporate later

---

## Name Options (all available in Delaware and Florida)
1. **TTI Fire Inc.** — short, clean, memorable
2. **TTI Fire Technologies Inc.** — signals tech focus
3. **TTI Fire Solutions Inc.** — strongest fit — covers all three pillars: Troubleshooting, Testing & Inspection + Monitoring

**TTI = Troubleshooting, Testing & Inspection** — the acronym already captures the core scope. "Solutions" as a suffix signals that the company delivers end-to-end value across all three — plus monitoring as the platform grows.

**Recommendation: TTI Fire Solutions Inc.** — strongest alignment with the full product scope.

---

## Next Steps (in order)
1. Agree on company name
2. File Delaware C-Corp (~$90 state fee + registered agent ~$50–150/year)
3. Register foreign corp in Florida (~$125)
4. Open business bank account
5. Enroll Apple Developer Organization account ($99/year) under company name

---

## Note on App Store / Mobile Deployment
- We can start iOS testing immediately under a **personal Apple Developer account** ($99/year) using TestFlight — no company name required, not visible to end users
- For the **public App Store release**, the Organization account must be active so the seller name shows as "TTI Fire Solutions Inc." — not a personal name
- App name, description, and branding are fully under our control regardless of account type
- Apps can be transferred between Apple Developer accounts, but the cleanest path is to have the Organization account ready before the first public App Store release
